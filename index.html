About

I am a PhD candidate in economics at the University of Zurich. My research is in empirical finance and housing economics, with a focus on how credit conditions and asset price dynamics shape investment behavior and the allocation of capital across the real economy.

I use administrative microdata — including Norwegian firm- and individual-level registers — to study questions at the intersection of household finance, macroprudential policy, and entrepreneurship.

Working Papers

Speculation and Capital Misallocation in Real Estate Booms
with [Coauthor Name]

Using Norwegian administrative data, we study how housing booms reshape entrepreneurial investment decisions, shifting capital from productive firm investment toward real estate. We document the magnitude of this reallocation and assess the role of tax incentives and credit availability in driving it.

The Distributional Effects of LTV Caps on Renters and Homebuyers
with Sebastian Doerr (BIS) and Gazi Kabaş (Tilburg University)

We estimate the effects of loan-to-value caps on housing market access and rental prices using a difference-in-differences design with Rambachan-Roth sensitivity analysis. We document heterogeneous effects across the income distribution and discuss implications for the design of macroprudential policy.
